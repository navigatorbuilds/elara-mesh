2. **S/Kademlia** (Baumgart & Mies, 2007) — crypto puzzles, parallel disjoint paths, 99% success
3. **Eclipse on Bitcoin** (Heilman et al., USENIX 2015) — defined attack class, test-before-evict
4. **Eclipse on Ethereum** (Marcus/Heilman/Goldberg, 2018) — 2 IPs to eclipse geth
5. **Eclipse on Ethereum Post-Merge** (Jan 2026, arXiv 2601.16560) — multi-stage DHT+DNS
6. **Sybil on IPFS** (ARES 2024) — single computer, 80% content denial
7. **Active Sybil on IPFS DHT** (2025, arXiv 2505.01139) — semantically correct false data
8. **Bitcoin BGP Hijacking** (ETH Zurich) — ISP-level partition attack
9. **Decentralized Hole Punching** (Seemann, Protocol Labs, 2022) — DCUtR design
10. **NAT Traversal Measurement** (Oct 2025, arXiv 2510.27500) — 70% success, 4.4M attempts
11. **Hierarchical DHT** (Springer) — two-tier gateway super-peers
12. **Harmony Whitepaper** — Kademlia-routed cross-shard, O(log N)
13. **Post-Quantum Crypto in Practice** (2025, IACR ePrint 2025/1668) — PQC deployment survey
